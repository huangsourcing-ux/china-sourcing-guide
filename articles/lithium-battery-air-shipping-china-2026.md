# Lithium Battery Air Shipping from China: 2026 Checklist

> Published: July 17, 2026
> Original: https://www.huangsourcing.com/lithium-battery-air-shipping-china-2026

Check lithium battery classification, 2026 state-of-charge rules, UN 38.3 evidence, packing, marks, and forwarder handoff before air shipping from China.

Before a dangerous-goods forwarder collects a China-sourced product with lithium batteries, the buyer should freeze the exact battery configuration, obtain qualified classification and transport instructions, and connect those instructions to state-of-charge evidence, UN 38.3 records, physical packing, required marks, and the booked air route.

## Quick Answer

The 2026 rule is not one blanket “30% for every device” instruction. IATA’s public guidance makes reduced state of charge mandatory for specified lithium-ion batteries packed with equipment under PI 966, while PI 967 batteries contained in equipment have a strong recommendation rather than the same mandate. Hold pickup until a qualified dangerous-goods partner confirms the classification and the China-side shipment matches the approved release file.

- [IATA - How to ship lithium batteries](https://www.iata.org/en/publications/newsletters/iata-knowledge-hub/what-to-know-about-how-to-ship-lithium-batteries/) — Primary IATA overview covering shipper responsibilities, air-waybill context, carrier contact, and availability of UN 38.3 test summaries.
- [FAA - Preventing lithium battery hazards](https://www.faa.gov/blog/clearedfortakeoff/case-preventing-lithium-battery-hazards) — Official current incident-pattern context for smoke, fire, and extreme-heat events in aviation; not a measure of China supplier failure rates.
- [NTSB - Brampton lithium-battery fire investigation](https://www.ntsb.gov/investigations/Pages/DCA16SH001.aspx) — Official public case finding that a short circuit led to thermal runaway and ignited a battery and its packaging, with combustible packing material contributing to the consequences.
- [Federal Register - 2026 PHMSA harmonization proposal](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2026-02-10/pdf/2026-02575.pdf) — Official proposed-rule record explaining the international risk basis and proposed U.S. harmonization; cited as a proposal, not a final U.S. rule.
